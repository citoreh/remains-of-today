Privacy Policy — What Remains of Today

Last updated: 1 July 2026

"What Remains of Today" (the "Extension") is a Chrome extension that displays a countdown of the time remaining in the current day, week, month, quarter, and year.

What data the Extension collects

None. The Extension does not collect, transmit, sell, or share any personal or sensitive user data. It has no host_permissions and makes no network requests of any kind — it does not read your browsing history, page content, tabs, or any other browser data.

What is stored, and where

The Extension stores only the settings you choose yourself — your preferred end-of-day time, accent color, badge style, and time zone selection — using Chrome's built-in chrome.storage.sync API. This data:

  • Never leaves your device via any server operated by the developer, because the developer operates no server.
  • Is synced only through Google's own Chrome Sync infrastructure, under your Google Account, exactly like your bookmarks or browser settings — the same way any Chrome extension using storage.sync works.
  • Can be cleared at any time by removing the extension or clearing its site data via chrome://extensions.

Permissions used

  • storage — to save your settings as described above.
  • alarms — to refresh the toolbar badge (the small percentage/hours indicator on the icon) once per minute.

Third parties

The Extension does not integrate any analytics, advertising, or third-party SDKs. No data is shared with any third party because no data is collected in the first place.

Limited Use disclosure

The use of information received from Google APIs by this Extension adheres to the Chrome Web Store User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements.

Changes to this policy

If this Extension ever begins collecting or transmitting data, this policy will be updated first, with a new "Last updated" date above, before that change ships.
