details

Built to stay out of the way.

Small choices that make it worth pinning to the toolbar instead of forgetting about.

A live badge on the icon

The toolbar icon shows the number itself — a percentage or an hour count, your choice — updated every minute. Most days you never need to click anything.

—% left today

Four accents

Amber, teal, violet, or a quiet monochrome. This whole page is wearing whichever one you pick.

Any end of day, anywhere

Midnight, 6pm, or whenever your day actually ends — in your local time zone or any other, from Tehran to Los Angeles.

Settings sync, not servers

Preferences travel through Chrome's own sync — the same as your bookmarks. There's no server of the developer's for anything to reach.

Personal alarms, your own colour

Add up to 8 personal countdowns — a launch, a flight, a deadline — each its own colored ring, separate from the built-in five. A Chrome notification tells you the moment one arrives.

Pick your week, hide the rest

Set your week to start Monday or Sunday. Then hide any ring you don't need — built-in or personal — one by one, so the toolbar only shows what matters to you.

the whole policy, honestly

Nothing is collected. Nothing is sent.

No analytics, no network requests, no account to make. The extension only ever calculates — using the clock your computer already has — and remembers the settings you choose, the way any Chrome extension does.

getting it

Install it in under a minute

Not on the Chrome Web Store yet — until then, this is genuinely the whole process.

  1. Download and unzipKeep the folder somewhere permanent — Chrome loads the extension from these files.
  2. Open chrome://extensionsPaste that into your address bar.
  3. Turn on Developer modeToggle in the top-right corner of that page.
  4. Click "Load unpacked"Select the unzipped folder — the one containing manifest.json.
  5. Pin itClick the puzzle-piece icon in your toolbar, then the pin next to "What Remains of Today".
